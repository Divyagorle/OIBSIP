A.P.J Abdul Kalam - A Tribute to India's Missile Man

Introduction:

APJ Abdul Kalam was a renowned scientist, statesman, and teacher who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, and grew up in a humble household. Kalam was widely regarded as the "People's President" for his down-to-earth nature and his commitment to education and youth development.

Early life and education:

Kalam was the youngest of four siblings and grew up in a close-knit family. His father was a boat owner and his mother was a homemaker. Despite facing financial difficulties, Kalam was a diligent student and was awarded a scholarship to study engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology. After completing his studies, he joined the Defence Research and Development Organization (DRDO) and later the Indian Space Research Organization (ISRO).

Career as a scientist and his contributions to India's missile program:

Kalam played a pivotal role in India's missile program and was instrumental in the development of the Agni and Prithvi missiles. He was also involved in India's civilian space program and served as the project director of India's first satellite launch vehicle, SLV-III.

Role as President of India from 2002 to 2007:

Kalam was elected as President of India in 2002 with overwhelming support from both ruling and opposition parties. During his tenure, he focused on education, youth development, and the promotion of a knowledge-based society. He was known for his interaction with students and his passion for igniting their creativity and innovation.

Personal life and hobbies:

Kalam was a deeply spiritual person and drew inspiration from the Bhagavad Gita. He was also an avid reader and enjoyed writing poetry in his spare time. Kalam was known for his simplicity and frugal lifestyle, and he often shared anecdotes from his childhood to inspire people to overcome adversity and pursue their dreams.

Legacy and impact on Indian society:

APJ Abdul Kalam is remembered as one of India's greatest leaders and visionaries. His dedication to education and youth development has inspired countless young people to pursue their dreams and make a positive impact on society. His message of hope, optimism, and perseverance continues to resonate with people around the world.


Quotes by APJ Abdul Kalam:

  • "We are all born with a divine fire in us. Our efforts should be to give wings to this fire and fill the world with the glow of its goodness."
  • "You have to dream before your dreams can come true."
